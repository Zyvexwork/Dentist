FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions? We have answers. Can't find what you're looking for? Call us or visit our contact page.

Is teeth cleaning painful?
Professional scaling and polishing is generally painless. You may feel mild sensitivity during the procedure, especially if you have inflamed gums. We offer a gentle anaesthetic rinse for sensitive patients, and our hygienists are trained to work as comfortably as possible.
How long does teeth whitening last?
In-chair professional whitening results typically last 12–18 months depending on your diet and habits (coffee, tea, smoking). Occasional top-up treatments with our take-home kit can extend results further. We provide detailed aftercare instructions with every whitening treatment.
At what age should my child first visit a dentist?
We recommend the first dental visit by age 1, or within 6 months of the first tooth appearing. Early visits are brief and relaxed — we focus on familiarising your child with the environment and giving parents advice on brushing, diet, and fluoride. Early dental habits make a lifelong difference!
How painful is a dental implant procedure?
Implant placement is performed under local anaesthesia, so you won't feel pain during the procedure. Afterward, most patients report mild discomfort similar to a tooth extraction — manageable with standard painkillers. Many patients are surprised by how comfortable the experience is.
Do you accept health insurance?
Yes, we accept all major health insurance plans including HDFC Ergo, Star Health, Niva Bupa, Care Health, Max Bupa, and Bajaj Allianz. We also offer cashless facility for most plans. Please bring your insurance card and policy details to your appointment, and our billing team will handle the rest.
How many sessions does root canal take?
With modern rotary endodontics, most root canals can be completed in a single 60–90 minute session. Complex cases with severe infection or multiple roots may require 2 visits. We'll give you a clear timeline during your initial consultation so you know exactly what to expect.
Can I get braces as an adult?
Absolutely! Age is no barrier to a straighter smile. We treat adults of all ages with metal braces, ceramic braces, or clear aligners. Clear aligners are particularly popular with adults as they're nearly invisible. The duration of treatment depends on your specific case — typically 12–24 months.
What should I do if I knock out a tooth?
Act fast! Pick up the tooth by the crown (not the root), rinse it gently with milk or saline — not water — and try to re-insert it in the socket. If that's not possible, keep it in milk or between your cheek and gum. Call us immediately — a tooth reimplanted within 30–60 minutes has the best chance of survival.
